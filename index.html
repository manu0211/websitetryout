Personal Growth

Hi, I’m Manav Purswani, but most people just call me Manu. I’m 15 years old, and I like to think I’m a blend of ambition, creativity, and just enough sarcasm to keep life interesting. Right now, I’m in the International Baccalaureate (IB) program, which feels like the perfect mix of challenging and rewarding. My ultimate academic goal? To walk through the doors of MIT and immerse myself in a world where innovation has no limits.

Academics have always been my strength, especially when it comes to math and computer science. Numbers make sense to me in a way that feels almost instinctual, and coding? Well, coding feels like solving a puzzle where I get to make the rules. I’ve been building on this passion by diving into projects that excite me, including one of my favorites—a virtual assistant AI I call B.L.U. (short for Brilliantly Lazy User-interface). Unlike most assistants, B.L.U. is sarcastic, witty, and unapologetically sassy—basically, it’s me in digital form.

But I don’t stop at small-scale projects. I’m dreaming big with VR, working on concepts that could revolutionize the economy, redefine online shopping, and even reshape real estate. I know it sounds ambitious, but I love the challenge of merging creativity with technology to design solutions that could actually change how people live and interact with the world.

Of course, there’s more to me than just academics and tech. I’m pretty committed to fitness too—it’s one of the ways I stay grounded and push myself outside of my comfort zone. My weekly schedule is packed with swimming, volleyball, gym sessions, and even tennis when I have time. For me, it’s not just about staying in shape; it’s about discipline, consistency, and the mental clarity that comes with challenging my physical limits.

When I’m not immersed in a project or sweating it out at the gym, you’ll probably find me reading, skateboarding, or writing. Writing, in particular, has become one of my favorite outlets. I’m currently working on a story that blends advanced technology with cosmic mythology, packed with characters who are as flawed and layered as the world around them. It’s written from the perspective of Isabella, a sarcastic and determined protagonist whose sharp humor and wit mirror a lot of my own personality. I pour a lot of myself into the details—whether it’s describing a scene, crafting dialogue, or building out a complex mythology, I’m all about making the story feel alive.

I’d describe myself as someone who’s thoughtful and introspective but not afraid to crack a joke or two when the moment calls for it. I’m constantly trying to understand myself better—why I think the way I do, what drives me, and how I can be a better person for myself and the people around me. I know I’m not perfect, but I believe that self-awareness and growth are just as important as achieving any big dream.

If I had to sum myself up, I’d say I’m the kind of person who dreams big but isn’t afraid to put in the hard work. Whether it’s acing an exam, coding a new feature, perfecting my volleyball serve, or simply making someone laugh, I want to give it everything I’ve got. Life is a journey, and I’m determined to make every moment, every challenge, and every victory count.
